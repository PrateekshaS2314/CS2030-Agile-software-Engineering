During this lab, we practiced Pair Programming by strictly following the Driver and Navigator roles. Initially, one member focused on writing the code while the other continuously reviewed the logic and suggested improvements. This helped us catch small mistakes immediately and improve the structure of our program. The Navigator helped think about edge cases, such as checking whether a course exists before enrolling a student, which improved the reliability of our code.
After switching roles, we both gained a better understanding of the complete system. Role switching helped us appreciate both perspectives — implementation and design. It also improved our communication and coordination skills. We discussed each step before writing the code, which reduced confusion and increased clarity.
Overall, Pair Programming made the development process more interactive and efficient. It reduced errors, improved code readability, and strengthened teamwork. This activity clearly demonstrated how collaboration and continuous feedback support Agile principles and lead to better software quality.
